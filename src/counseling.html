What is Mental Health?

There are many ways to define mental health. To different people, it can mean a lot of different things.
Here at L.E.A.D, we view mental health as just one piece of a person’s overall health.
This means there is no “health” without “mental health.”
Mental health is more than just not having mental illness - mental health is an important resource for everyday life.
It affects how we feel, think and understand the world.
Good mental health and well-being helps us to stay balanced and be resilient, to enjoy life and cope with everyday stress, and to bounce back from bigger setbacks.

Mental health challenges and disorders have many different signs and symptoms and can look different in different people. They can impact how a person thinks, feels and behaves. Some common symptoms of a mental health challenge or disorder are:

  • changes in mood
  • changes in the way you perceive things
  • obsessions
  • fears
  • feelings of anxiety
